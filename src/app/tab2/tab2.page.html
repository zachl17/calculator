History of the Calculator The first modernized calculator
  • Curt Herzstark invented the first handheld, mechanic calculator in 1945.
  • His calculators were produced in large quantities until 1970, when a company in Japan invented the first digital pocket calculator.
  • Companies in the United States, such as Texas Instruments, adapted the design of the Japanese device, and enhanced it by creating the graphing calculators we know today.
 The scientific calculator
  • Designed to calculate problems in science, engineering, and mathematics.
  • The defining features of a scientific calculator include: scientific notation, floating-point arithmetic, and logarithmic functions.
